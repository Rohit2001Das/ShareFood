Our Mission

Our mission is to make use of excess food from San Francisco restaurants by providing a platform for restaurants to reach charities and non-profits in the area. Restaurants are easily able to make an offer to a charity on our website. This way restaurants and charities can connect with each other. Once a restaurant fills in the form, they can contact a local organization and send a request to share food. The charity/organization can then accept the offer. Ultimately, we aim to help those in need in our community by providing food, while also reducing food waste.

About

According to the 2018 Annual Homeless Assessment Report (AHAR) to Congress, "68.9% of California’s Homeless Community were left unsheltered in 2018, being the highest percentage of any state, and according to the San Francisco Chronicle, more than 1 in 10 people in the Bay Area today are struggling to find their next meal". The article states, "The populations most impacted by hunger are also the most vulnerable members of society: the Bay Area’s children and senior citizens. Even in Silicon Valley, one in three children — about 200,000 — are food insecure, according to Second Harvest Food Bank, which services Santa Clara and San Mateo counties." Furthermore, as the non-profit organization reFED explained, "The excess food that remains unused by restaurants at the end of the day produces HALF A POUND of food waste." That adds up to 11.4 MILLION tons and 25 BILLION dollars worth of food waste per year."
